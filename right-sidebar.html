Immersive Audio

This project, led by Connor Safran, a graduate of the Electroacoustic Music Studio at Hanns Eisler Berlin, focused on immersive audio design and live electronic performance techniques, by controlling an 8.1 channel Genelec system to create surround sound experiences.

Production

In the first part of the project I rebuild a Algorithm for a spatial audio panner tool to control sound placement in 3D space by calculating the volume of each speaker based on the distance to a virtual audio source to fully understand its core functionality. This process involved creating an interface to make key parameters accessible.

I worked with Carlos Luis Strohm to explore generative music and sound processing using both Max and vvvv. We developed various audio generators and effects, and interconected them to realive motion and acceleration data captured by an **M5 microcontroller.
