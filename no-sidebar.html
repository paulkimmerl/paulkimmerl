NEW NOW Festivall 2023

NEW NOW is a leading European festival for digital art. In its second edition, held from June 1 to August 6, 2023, the UNESCO World Heritage Site Zollverein became a hub for showcasing and creating digital artworks. The festival, themed "Hypernatural Forces," explored the interplay between nature and digital technology, blurring the boundaries between reality and simulation.

MXL#15: SOUNDS OF SILENCE

IMXL#15 SOUNDS OF SILENCE, led by Darsha Hewitt, focused on the socio-ecological impact of sound, combining theoretical research with hands-on experimentation in sound and technology. The project began with workshops on DIY electronic instruments, including contact microphones and soldering, and an introduction to sound system culture and sound clashes. Participants were tasked with building a karaoke machine that integrated pre-recorded tracks, vocal effects, and interactive elements, addressing socio-political themes.

Production

The project unfolded through several phases. Initially, we conducted sound walks, captured sound sculptures, and explored sound sustainability. The midterm phase focused on developing a concept paper, which was shared with a partner course in Montreal to inform their short-term projects. After refining our concepts, we moved into an intense production phase, building and programming the prototype karaoke machine.

The team presented their prototypes at the NEW NOW Festival in Essen, where the project was tested in a professional setting. The installation incorporated custom-built mechanical suits, real-time visuals, and sound manipulation via a rotary speaker and tape delay system. Following the festival, the group re-evaluated the machine, evolving its design and functionality before the final showcase.
